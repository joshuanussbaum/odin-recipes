Char Siu (Chinese BBQ Pork)

This sticky, sweet pork recipe is a perfect addition to your regular cooking repertoire - with plenty of leftovers that taste great with rice or noodles for any meal of the day!

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds Boneless Pork Shoulder or Pork Butt
  • 1/4 cup Granulated White Sugar
  • 2 teaspoons Salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon Five Spice Powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon White Pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon Sesame Oil
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing Rice Wine
  • 1 tablespoon Soy Sauce
  • 1 Tablespoon Hoising Sauce
  • 2 teaspoons Molasses
  • 3 cloves Garlic, Minced
  • 2 tablespoons Honey
  • 1 tablespoon Hot Water

Steps

  1. Cut the pork into strips or chunks about 2-3 inches thick, leaving fat on.
  2. In a small or medium sized bowl, combine sugar, salt, five spice, white pepper, sesame oil, rice wine, soy sauce, hoisin sauce, molasses, and minced garlic.
  3. Reserve about 2 tablespoons of marinade, then in a large bowl or baking dish rub the pork with the remaining marinade. Cover and refrigerate for a minimum of 8 hours (overnight works best). Do the same for the reserved marinade.
  4. Place rack in the upper third of your oven, and preheat oven to 475F (245C).
  5. Line a sheet pan with foil and place a metal rack on top. Pour 1 1/2 cups water into the pan. Place the pork on rack, leaving as much space between pieces as possible.
  6. Transfer pork to the preheated oven. Roast for 25 minutes, reducing the oven temperature to 375F (190C) after 10 minutes.
  7. Flip the pork, and carefully rotate the pan 180 degrees. If the bottom of the pan is dry, add another cup of water. Roast for another 15 minutes.
  8. Meanwhile, combine the reserved marinade with honey and 1 tablespoon hot water.
  9. After 40 minutes of total roasting time, baste the pork, flip it, and baste the other side. Roast for 10 minutes.
  10. For crispy pork, broil for no more than 5 minutes. Pork is ready when it looks sticky and caramelized, but not burnt. Internal temperature should read at least 145F (62C).
  11. Remove from oven and baste with any leftover marinade. Rest for 10 minutes, slice, and serve.
